# Structural Primitives Palette

A working vocabulary of terms that carry identical structural meaning across all domains — code, trading, physics, music, mathematics, philosophy, game theory, agent orchestration. These are not analogies. A **worker** in a web framework and a **worker** in physics and a **worker** in employment perform the same structural role: an entity that converts input into output through effort. No definition needed. No translation needed.

These primitives are the shared protocol between a human and an LLM. They compress complex instructions into single words that both parties resolve identically.

---

## Entities That Act

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **actor** | Anything that initiates change. Cinema, physics, game theory, software — same structural role everywhere. Has agency, takes action, produces effects. |
| **agent** | An actor with autonomy and goals. Distinct from actor because it implies decision-making capacity. |
| **worker** | An entity that performs work (physics: force × displacement; software: task execution; social: labor). Always implies input → effort → output. |
| **observer** | An entity that extracts information without (ideally) perturbing the system. Quantum mechanics, design patterns, market analysis — same role. |
| **operator** | An entity or function that transforms one thing into another. Math, telephony, machinery, surgery — always means "the thing that acts on something." |
| **player** | An actor within a rule-governed system. Game theory makes this precise, but it's intuitive everywhere: someone with a strategy, facing other players, within constraints. |

## What They Act On

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **state** | The complete description of a system at a moment. Thermodynamics, FSMs, mental health, political science — always means "where things are right now." |
| **signal** | Information carried through a medium. Electrical, social, market, neural — always means "a detectable change that conveys meaning." |
| **token** | A discrete unit extracted from a continuous stream. Linguistics, authentication, LLMs, transit — always means "a bounded piece that represents something larger." |
| **object** | A bounded thing with properties. Philosophy, OOP, physics, grammar — always means "a thing you can point at and describe." |
| **resource** | Something consumed or allocated to enable work. CPU, capital, attention, raw materials — same structural role everywhere. |
| **message** | A discrete unit of communication between actors. Queues, conversations, protocols, diplomacy — always means "a packaged signal with a sender and intended receiver." |
| **payload** | The content carried by a message, distinct from its envelope/metadata. Rockets, HTTP, espionage — same structure. |

## How Things Connect

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **interface** | The contact surface between two systems. API, UI, membrane, diplomatic protocol — always means "how two things touch without merging." |
| **boundary** | Where one thing ends and another begins. Cell walls, property lines, module boundaries, Bolzano-Weierstrass — always means "the edge of a domain." |
| **channel** | A constrained path for flow. Rivers, TV, ion channels, communication theory — always means "a medium shaped to direct movement." |
| **bridge** | A structure that connects two otherwise separated things. Infrastructure, music, dentistry, networking — always means "a thing that spans a gap." |
| **coupling** | The degree to which two things depend on each other. Mechanical, software, chemical, relational — always means "how much pulling one affects the other." |
| **binding** | A commitment that links two things. Chemical bonds, legal contracts, variable binding, bookbinding — always means "a connection that constrains future freedom." |
| **medium** | The substance through which signals or forces propagate. Air, water, culture, network — always means "what carries the transmission." |
| **port** | A point of entry/exit. Harbors, USB, wine, I/O — always means "where things cross a boundary." |

## How Things Are Organized

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **layer** | A horizontal stratum in a stack. Geology, networking (OSI), cake, abstraction — always means "one level in a vertical arrangement." |
| **domain** | A bounded region of authority or relevance. Geography, DNS, mathematics, expertise — always means "the space where certain rules apply." |
| **scope** | The region within which a name or rule is valid. Variables, jurisdiction, telescopes, project scope — always means "how far something reaches." |
| **namespace** | A container that prevents name collisions. DNS, programming, taxonomy, filing systems — always means "a labeled scope." |
| **hierarchy** | A tree-structured ordering by rank or containment. Organizations, file systems, biology, feudalism — always means "nested levels of authority or inclusion." |
| **set** | A collection of distinct elements. Mathematics, tennis, dishes, music — and in the mathematical sense, every person intuitively understands "the set of things that..." without any formal training. |
| **subset** | A set contained within another set. Equally intuitive — "a subset of our customers" needs no mathematical disclaimer. |
| **category** | A grouping by shared structure. Aristotle, grocery aisles, category theory — always means "things that belong together by some criterion." |
| **zone** | A region with distinct rules or properties. Time zones, war zones, your zsh three-zone architecture — always means "a space where different conditions apply." |
| **stack** | An ordered accumulation where the last thing added is the first removed. Pancakes, call stacks, technology stacks — the structure is always the same. |
| **queue** | The opposite ordering: first in, first out. Lines, print queues, message queues — same everywhere. |

## How Things Change

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **transition** | Movement from one state to another. Phase transitions, CSS transitions, life transitions — always means "the process of becoming the next state." |
| **transformation** | A mapping that changes form while preserving some structure. Math, biology, personal growth, data pipelines — always means "change that follows rules." |
| **mutation** | A change to internal state. Genetics, programming, virus variants — always means "alteration of what something is, not just where it is." |
| **evolution** | Iterative change through selection pressure. Biology, language, markets, design — always means "gradual improvement through feedback and variation." |
| **drift** | Gradual undirected change. Genetic drift, continental drift, scope drift, semantic drift — always means "slow movement without intentional force." |
| **decay** | Loss of structure or energy over time. Radioactive, dental, social, signal — always means "degradation toward disorder." |
| **cascade** | A chain reaction where one event triggers the next. Waterfalls, CSS, failures, neural activation — always means "sequential amplification." |
| **cycle** | A sequence that returns to its starting point. Carbon cycle, business cycle, wash cycle, circadian — always means "a loop in time." |
| **oscillation** | Repeated movement between two states. Pendulums, markets, moods, AC current — always means "back-and-forth around a center." |

## How Things Stay the Same

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **invariant** | A property that survives transformation. Physics conservation laws, loop invariants, cultural constants — always means "the thing that doesn't change when everything else does." |
| **identity** | The property that makes a thing itself. Personal, mathematical (a × 1 = a), authentication — always means "what remains when you strip away context." |
| **equilibrium** | A state where opposing forces balance. Chemistry, economics, game theory, ecology — always means "the point of rest under tension." |
| **conservation** | The principle that some quantity is preserved through change. Energy, mass, momentum, trust — always means "you can't create or destroy this, only move it." |
| **homeostasis** | Active maintenance of a stable state despite perturbation. Biology, thermostats, central banking — always means "a system fighting to stay where it is." |
| **contract** | A mutual agreement that constrains future behavior. Legal, API, social, design-by-contract — always means "a promise that enables trust." |

## How Things Grow and Spread

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **emergence** | Macro behavior arising from micro rules. Consciousness, flocking, market prices, Conway's Game of Life — always means "the whole does something the parts don't." |
| **amplification** | Increasing the magnitude of a signal. Audio, biological, rhetorical, financial leverage — always means "making a small thing bigger." |
| **resonance** | When a system's natural frequency matches an input frequency, producing disproportionate response. Bridges, music, marketing, emotional — always means "alignment that multiplies effect." |
| **diffusion** | Spreading from high concentration to low concentration. Molecules, ideas, innovation, heat — always means "movement toward uniform distribution." |
| **propagation** | Transmission through a medium. Light, sound, errors, rumors — always means "a signal moving forward through space or structure." |
| **feedback** | Output routed back as input. Audio, biology, management, control systems — always means "the system hearing itself." Positive feedback amplifies; negative feedback stabilizes. |
| **network effect** | Value increasing with the number of participants. Telephones, social platforms, languages, standards — always means "more users = more value per user." |
| **contagion** | Spread through contact. Disease, emotion, financial panic, memes — always means "proximity causes transmission." |

## How Things Constrain

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **threshold** | A value below which nothing happens and above which everything changes. Action potentials, boiling points, tipping points, minimum viable — always means "the line between inert and active." |
| **bottleneck** | The narrowest point that limits total throughput. Traffic, production, software, digestion — always means "the constraint that governs the whole." |
| **filter** | A mechanism that passes some things and blocks others. Coffee, email, signal processing, hiring — always means "selective permeability." |
| **gate** | A binary control point: open or closed. Logic gates, garden gates, gating mechanisms in neurons — always means "a point that permits or denies passage." |
| **limit** | A bound that can be approached but not exceeded (or a value approached asymptotically). Speed of light, rate limits, mathematical limits, human limits — always means "the edge of what's possible." |
| **friction** | Resistance to movement between surfaces in contact. Physical, UX, organizational, market — always means "the cost of doing the next thing." |
| **pressure** | Force per unit area; or more broadly, sustained force demanding response. Atmospheric, social, deadline, peer — always means "a force that compresses or compels." |
| **tension** | Opposing forces pulling apart. Strings, narrative, organizational, mechanical — always means "stress from being pulled in two directions." |

## How You Navigate

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **path** | A connected sequence of steps from origin to destination. Filesystem, career, graph theory, hiking — always means "the route through a space." |
| **gradient** | The direction and rate of steepest change. Temperature, machine learning, color, elevation — always means "which way is uphill and how steep." |
| **landscape** | A surface over a space where height represents some value. Fitness landscapes, career landscapes, actual landscapes — always means "the topology of possibilities." |
| **trajectory** | The path actually taken through a space over time. Ballistics, career, orbital mechanics, project — always means "where you went, not just where you could go." |
| **orbit** | A trajectory that cycles. Planets, electrons (loosely), habits, recurring meetings — always means "a path that comes back." |
| **map** | A structure-preserving representation of a territory. Cartography, category theory, mental models, function mapping — always means "a smaller thing that shows you a bigger thing." |
| **compass** | An instrument or principle that provides orientation. Navigation, moral compass, design principles — always means "the thing that tells you which direction matters." |

## How You Decide

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **strategy** | A plan that accounts for other actors' responses. Military, game theory, business, chess — always means "a contingent plan in a competitive space." |
| **option** | The right but not the obligation to act. Finance, software options pattern, life choices — always means "preserved future freedom." |
| **hedge** | A position that reduces risk from another position. Finance, gardening (hedgerow), language hedging — always means "protection against downside." |
| **position** | Where you stand in a space. Chess, trading, argument, physical — always means "your current location relative to others." |
| **leverage** | A small input producing a large output through mechanical advantage. Physics, finance, negotiation, software — always means "force multiplication." |
| **payoff** | The outcome received for a given action. Game theory, investment, effort, gambling — always means "what you get for what you did." |
| **commitment** | An action that reduces your own future options to gain credibility. Game theory, relationships, architecture decisions — always means "burning bridges to prove intent." |
| **arbitrage** | Exploiting price differences across markets. Finance, information, regulatory, attention — always means "profiting from inconsistency." |

## How You See

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **signal** | (Repeated from above because it belongs here too.) Detectable information in a medium. |
| **noise** | Everything in a medium that isn't signal. Static, market noise, irrelevant data, background chatter — always means "what you have to see through." |
| **resolution** | The fineness of distinction. Pixels, conflict resolution, scientific instruments, decision granularity — always means "how small a difference you can detect." |
| **perspective** | The viewpoint from which observation occurs. Art, philosophy, relativity, stakeholder — always means "where you're standing changes what you see." |
| **frame** | A bounded context that determines interpretation. Picture frames, reference frames, framing effects, window frames — always means "the border that gives meaning to what's inside." |
| **lens** | A tool that changes how you see. Optics, analytical frameworks, cultural lens — always means "a thing you look through that reshapes the image." |
| **pattern** | A recurring regularity. Textile, behavioral, mathematical, design — always means "structure that repeats." |
| **spectrum** | A continuous range between two extremes. Light, political, autism, radio — always means "a dimension with poles." |

## How Things Compose (The Meta-Primitives)

| Primitive | Why it's universal |
|---|---|
| **composition** | Combining parts into wholes where the result is itself a valid part. Functions, music, photography, materials — always means "putting things together such that the result plays by the same rules." |
| **decomposition** | Breaking a whole into parts that can be understood independently. Chemistry, music, project management, Fourier — always means "taking apart without destroying." |
| **abstraction** | Hiding detail to expose only what matters at a given level. Programming, art, mathematics, management — always means "forgetting on purpose." |
| **projection** | Mapping from a higher-dimensional space to a lower one. Geometry, psychology, budgeting, cinema — always means "collapsing dimensions to see a shadow of the whole." |
| **isomorphism** | A structure-preserving bijection. Math, cross-domain thinking, translation — always means "different surface, same bones." |
| **duality** | Two perspectives on the same underlying thing. Wave/particle, time/frequency, buyer/seller, syntax/semantics — always means "two valid descriptions that are secretly one." |
| **recursion** | Self-reference; a thing defined in terms of itself. Fractals, directories, jokes, mathematical induction — always means "the part contains a version of the whole." |
| **symmetry** | Invariance under transformation. Physics, art, algebra, faces — always means "you can change something and it looks the same." |
| **transduction** | Converting one form of energy or signal into another. Biology, electronics, metaphor, data pipelines — always means "same information, different medium." |

## Using This Palette

These primitives function as a shared instruction set. When used in skill descriptions, agent prompts, business logic specifications, or development process documentation:

**They compress.** "Add a gate before the worker that filters by threshold" is one sentence that any LLM resolves correctly without elaboration.

**They compose.** The primitives combine: "a feedback loop that amplifies signal above a threshold and dampens noise below it" — every word here is a primitive, and the sentence builds a complete system specification from them.

**They transfer.** A skill written using these terms works identically whether applied to a trading strategy, a data pipeline, a zsh module, or an agent orchestration flow. The vocabulary is the portability layer.

**They don't need definitions.** That's the test. If you have to explain what the word means in context, it's not a primitive — it's jargon. Every word in this palette passes the test: a farmer, a physicist, a programmer, and a trader all resolve it to the same structural meaning.

### For Skill Creation
Write skill descriptions using these primitives. An LLM reading "this skill transforms raw input through a pipeline of filters, producing tokens that compose into structured output" knows exactly what to build without any domain-specific explanation.

### For Agent Orchestration
Name your agents, channels, and protocols with these words. An agent named `observer` that watches a `channel` for `signals` above a `threshold` and sends a `message` to a `worker` — that architecture is self-documenting.

### For Business Logic
Model your processes as interactions between `players` with `strategies`, mediated by `contracts`, constrained by `boundaries`, navigating a `landscape` of `options`. Game theory becomes operational vocabulary, not academic framework.

### For Development Process
Your workflow is a `pipeline` with `gates`. Your code review is a `filter`. Your CI is a `feedback loop`. Your monorepo `zones` have `interfaces`. Your modules have `contracts`. Your refactoring reduces `coupling`. None of this is metaphor — it's precise structural description.
